Concept

For the past two years, an interdisciplinary group has been exploring how to weave together an undisciplined mesh of Design, Art, and Technology — spanning countries, institutions, and generations, consistently framed through the lens of media art.

It connects a new English-language PhD programme in Tbilisi with a festival and exhibition format that opens up artistic research beyond the academic sphere and into the public realm.

From street art to sound and memorial art to immersive projections — all opening up space for reflection on memory, power, technology, and perception.

HOSTILE NOISE exists in the tension, the clash, the interference. It thrives where art, design, and technology collide — deliberately generating friction, disruption, and critical reflection. We reject neutrality, comfort, and institutional smoothness. Knowledge is never pure; media is never innocent; perception is never passive.
